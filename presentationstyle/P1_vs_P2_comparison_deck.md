THE DECISIVE ASYMMETRY

WHAT'S ACTUALLY ON THE MARKET
H₂
Large H-class CCGT (GE 9HA.02): 64.0% net on gas, 50% H₂ now, roadmap to 100%.
NH₃
100% ammonia only commercialised on a 40 MW-class machine (MHI H-25, ~2025): ~51% GTCC.
⇒
The ~13-point turbine gap outweighs the ~11-point cracking penalty. Hydrogen wins because of the engine it unlocks.
Sources: GE Vernova / Turbomachinery Magazine (9HA.02);  Mitsubishi Power / MHPS (H-25, world-first 100% NH₃ 40MW GT).
