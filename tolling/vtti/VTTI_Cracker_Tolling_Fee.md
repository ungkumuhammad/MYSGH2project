2.

Indicative Tariff

VTTI intends to structure an integrated tariff for the Project’s ammonia
storage and cracking services. The indicative integrated tariff consists of
(i) a take-or-pay component, payable monthly in advance, and (ii) a
variable component that reflects pass-through costs aligned with actual
usage of the Project facilities (for example, costs related to natural gas,
green electricity, other utility flows, and the EU Emissions Trading
Scheme tariff for greenhouse gas emissions). This tariff covers key
services at the Project facilities, including temporary ammonia storage,
ammonia cracking by the Project, and delivery of hydrogen produced
from the cracked ammonia into the hydrogen network.

FEED phase cost estimates associated with an ammonia cracker capable

intensity thresholds for renewable and low-carbon hydrogen, and
therefore provides the following indicative carbon intensities for
hydrogen delivered via the Project. Based on independent modelling by
CertifHy and Hinicio using pre-FEED inputs, the expected ranges of
carbon intensity are as follows:

4.

Indicative Yield

Yield depends on various factors, including the selection of cracker
technology and the choice of fuel employed in the cracking process. VTTI
has prepared the Project to accommodate a dual-fuel solution from two
remaining technology providers; the final selection will be made prior to
signing of the Heads of Agreement. By way of indicative guidance, where
natural gas is maximised as the heat source, the ratio is roughly 6.3–6.5
tonnes of NH₃ per tonne of H₂. Maximising green ammonia as the heat
source leads to a ratio of approximately 7.1–7.3 tonnes of NH₃ per tonne
of H₂.
