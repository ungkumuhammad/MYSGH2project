1 GENERAL
This document describes the process scheme and the main general control philosophy for the
Ammonia Cracking Unit (ACU) to produce Hydrogen from storage Ammonia.
Simple loop control systems are not described since they can be considered as per normal
engineering design practices and commonly known.
The plant is designed for the following main nominal capacities referred to Hydrogen Product,
99.5 %wt H purity:

 CASE 2:24 ktpa
 CASE 3:68 ktpa
 CASE 4:80 ktpa.

 CASE A -100% Natural Gas Fuel

3 LIQUID AMMONIA VAPORIZATION AND PREHEATING
SECTION
Liquid Ammonia is supplied at Battery Limit at 5bargpressureminimum and-33°Cand sentto a
dedicated ammonia feed drum.
The liquid ammonia feed outlet from the ammonia feed drum is then pumped up to the required
pressure and then preheated and finally vaporized by recovering heat available from the hot
cracked gas.
During start-up the Ammonia feed heating is achieved by a dedicated Ammonia Feed Heater
System which foresees a small electric Ammonia Feed Heater to accelerate the start-up
operation.
Afterward the vaporized ammonia is preheated up to the temperature required for the reaction
before to be fed to the Ammonia cracking Section where the ammonia cracking reaction takes
place.

As the ammonia cracking reaction is an equilibrium reaction, the cracked gas leaving the catalyst
tubes still contains a very small content of un-reacted ammonia.
The endothermicity of the reaction is sustained by the heat of combustion of a proper amount of
fuel.
The Ammonia Cracking Unit is mainly composed by a combination of Adiabatic Cracker Reactors
and an Ammonia Cracking Furnace, SMR based, which configuration is selected according to the
required plant capacity and performances.
The fuel gas oftheAmmonia Cracking Furnacewill be different for the three alternatives required.
o CASE A: the fuel gas consists of Natural Gas as main fuel and tail gas (or waste gas)
from the H2 purification Unit (PSA)
o CASE B: fuel gas consists of a mix of 50% of Natural Gas and 50% of cracked gas as
main fuel and tail gas (or waste gas) from the H2 purification Unit (PSA)
o CASE C: the fuel gas consists of a fixed amount of cracked gas and tail gas (or waste
gas) from the H2 purification Unit (PSA) as main fuel while a small quantity of hydrogen
product is used as make-up fuel for control purpose.
The heat recovery system forall thealternatives has been provided with a dedicated Combustion
Air Preheater which maximize the heat recovery in the flue gas system minimizing the total fuel
requirement and consequently increasing the overall efficiency.

8 PROCESS CONTROL AND SAFEGUARDING PHILOSOPHY:
GENERAL PRINCIPLES.
The safeguarding philosophy for the ammonia cracking plant prioritizes a layered approach to
safety, focusing on prevention, mitigation, and emergency response. This includes process
control, safety instrumentation, and robust safety systemsand measures like ventilation systems,
sensors for leak detection, and thorough employee training, alongside a culture of safety
awareness, to minimize the risk of incidents like leaks, fires, and explosions.
The control system will be designed to provide safe plant operation and controlled shutdown in
the event of a process upset or equipment failure (using the last good value or putting the
controller in manual mode). An automatic shutdown system, the Interlock System, will be
incorporated to protect personnel and equipment against possible damages.
The plant control can be considered as having one main control area: the Combustion Control.
A strict combustion control is applied to maintain the cracking furnace temperature within design
limits whilst achieving efficient combustion with a controlled excess of air within the ammonia
cracking furnace box.
The control system will act also as a Capacity Controller of the plant, i.e. it will determine the
setpoints of the main operating parameters for the ammonia cracking section based on the
desired value of capacity, inputted by operators.
All the other conventional automatic controllers installed in the plant will allow increasing the
production in all sections without a direct intervention of the operators.

In particular, the Capacity Controller will automatically select the setpoints of the ammonia feed
and fuel flowrates and the combustion air flow on the basis of the capacity level specified by
operators. When all these parameters are fixed, the automatic controllers of compressors,
columns, exchanger bypasses and separators level will allow these equipment operating at the
required capacity.
The plant interlock system aims to maintain a safe and reliable environment in the plant area
protecting personnel, equipment and machinery against malfunctioning of the control system
and/or human mistakes.
Interlock logics can be grouped as follows:
I. Main Interlocks Logic (E)
II. Partial Interlocks Logic (I)
III. Interlocks PLC Logic (P)
The first set (I) includes all interlocks that lead to entire plant section shutdown and/or main
machines shutdown up to the general plant shutdown. Apart of general and plant section shut-
down situation, above mentioned, it is also possible to have a partial shut-down (II), limited to the
operating conditions of individual equipment or to the feeding of some streams to section of the
plant. We call them as Partial Interlocks.
Finally, the last interlocks group includes interlock logics implemented in Vendor Unit PLC system
and integrated in the plant ESD system.
The most important PLC system in the Ammonia cracking Unit is represented by the Ammonia
Cracking Furnace PLC. Its main Interlock Logics can be grouped as follows:
i. Main Interlocks Furnace Logic (P) (implemented in the BMS PLC)
ii. Partial InterlocksFurnaceLogic (P) (implemented in the BMS PLC)
iii. BMS Logic (implemented in the BMS PLC)
The sets (i) and (ii) include all ESD interlocks that lead to entire Furnace package unit shutdown
and/or main machines/section shutdown and for this reason are to be implemented in the plant
BMS PLC.
The main Furnace DCS Interlockslogicswill keep the unit in a safe operating condition by means
the plant control system (DCS). These logics are activated by Ammonia Furnace package
external upset and for this reason it is admitted maintaining the Ammonia Cracking Furnace in
operation in a safe condition: in the meantime this operation is supervised by the ESD logics
(implemented in the BMS PLC).
The last set (iii) is the BMS itself that manages the burners lighting sequence up to the reset of
all the ESD logics as described in the relevant section. All the logics will be implemented in a
dedicated BMS PLC which will be integrated with the ESD PLC in one integrated SIL3 certified
fail safe and redundant system with separate controllers.
The interlock system will be provided with proper Reset Philosophy, Process Overrides (POS)
and Maintenance Overrides (MOS).
DCS and ESD systems must be fully separately and independent both HW and SW functions.
Both systems shall work integrated with one data base, but separate architecture complies with
standards for physical and logical separation and independence of safety and control.
