2.1.1. MACH ™ ADIABATIC AMMONIA CRACKER
2
The proposed technology for the adiabatic ammonia cracker is based on the CASALE proprietary
"Axial-Radial" design with open top catalyst beds.
FIG. 3: CASALE AXIAL-RADIAL DISTRIBUTION CONCEPT
CASALE axial-radial REACTOR concept has the following advantages:
- Utilizes efficiently the full volume of the catalytic beds, including the top layer. This is
possible thanks to the uniform gas velocity throughout the bed insuring the best catalyst
utilization on any cylindrical bed section.
- Easier mechanical construction: a completely sealed top cover for the catalytic bed is
not required.
Furthermore, a fluid-dynamic analysis is performed on the reactor so designed, in order to
ensure that the proper axial and the radial flow distribution in the catalytic bed is established.
2.1.1.1. AXIAL-RADIAL TECHNOLOGY ADVANTAGES
The axial-radial design has several advantages over the axial reactors.
A. No possibilities of channeling
The gas distribution in the catalyst bed is independent from catalyst loading, age and
condition, since it is controlled by the perforated walls and not by the catalyst itself. This
ensures perfect distribution and full utilization of the bed in all operating conditions and
loads, and independent from catalyst poor distribution, ensuring a longer life for the
same catalyst volume.

B. Low pressure drop.
The lower gas velocity of the axial-radial flow in the catalyst, compared with the axial
bed, ensures a low pressure drop even in case of unexpected events, as for instance,
powder or water droplets dragging.
The pressure drop of the axial-radial reactor internals can be optimized and properly
designed as it is almost entirely located in the perforated wall.
C. Possibility to use of small-size catalyst
The axial-radial reactor allows using smaller size catalyst with no detrimental effects on
pressure drop. It is well known that in any pore-diffusion limited reaction, the apparent
catalyst activity is inversely proportional to pellets dimensions, since only the outer
portion of the pellet tends to be used. The small-size catalyst is more active than the
large-size one, thanks to its higher surface per unit of volume, and this higher activity
facilitates a longer catalyst life for the same loaded volume.
Casale has already adopted this solution in several similar applications and has provided
excellent performance and reliable operation even after several years of operation.

MACH ™ ammonia cracking furnace is composed of a multi parallel catalyst filled tubes located

The selected furnace configuration for the MACH ™ Technology is the "top-fired" one, which
2

The general arrangement of the MACH ™ ammonia cracking furnace foresees the following
2
main sections:
A. RADIANT SECTION
The radiant section is the place where the ammonia is decomposed to hydrogen and nitrogen
through the endothermic ammonia cracking reaction.
The rate at which this reaction proceeds in the tubes is determined by the catalyst activity and
by the heat transfer rate from the combustion chamber to the tubes.
The design of the radiant section is therefore a complicate exercise where a specific
combination of mechanical expertise, kinetics know-how and knowledge of radiant and
convective heat transfer is absolutely required.
The catalyst tubes will be required to operate at very severe operating conditions both in terms
of temperature and pressure and in environment rich in NH and H .
